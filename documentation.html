Sundial

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

A sundial measure time by the position ofthe sun. The most commonly seen designs, such
as the 'ordinary' or standard garden sundial, cast shadow on a flat surface marked with the hours of
the day. As the position of the sun changes, the time indicated by the shadow changes. However,
sundials can be designed for any surface where a fixed object casts a predictable shadow.

Most sundial designs indicate apparent solar time. Minor design variation can measures standard and daylight saving time, as well.

History

Sundial in the form of obelisks (3500 BC) and shadow clocks (1500 BC) are known from ancient Egypt,
and were developed further by other cultures, including the chinese, Greek, and Roman cultures.
A type of sundial witout gnomon is described in the old Tesriment (Isaiah 38:2)
